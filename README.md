# Apple Retail Sales & Warranty Analytics using PostgreSQL

## Project Overview

This project analyzes a large-scale Apple retail sales and warranty dataset using PostgreSQL. The goal was to uncover business insights related to store performance, product sales, warranty behavior, product lifecycle trends, and data quality issues through advanced SQL analysis.

The dataset contains over 1 million sales records distributed across five relational tables representing stores, products, categories, sales transactions, and warranty claims.

In addition to business analysis, multiple data validation audits were performed to identify and isolate anomalies that could impact downstream reporting and KPI calculations.

## Data Quality Audits & Anomaly Detection

### 1. Product Launch Validation

A data quality audit was performed to identify transactions where products were sold before their official launch date.

Validation Rule:

```sql
sale_date < launch_date
```

#### Finding
A significant number of records were identified where the recorded sale date preceded the product launch date. Investigation suggested these inconsistencies originated from synthetic data generation limitations within the source dataset.
---

### 2. Warranty Chronology Validation

A second validation audit examined warranty claim chronology.

Validation Rule:

```sql
claim_date < sale_date
```

#### Finding

A total of **2,687 logical anomalies** were identified where warranty claims were recorded before the associated sales transaction.

Investigation suggested these records resulted from synthetic data generation inconsistencies rather than valid business events.

To maintain analytical accuracy, warranty-related calculations were refactored using strict chronological validation:

```sql
WHERE claim_date >= sale_date
```

This prevented invalid records from impacting warranty KPIs and claim-duration calculations.

## Key Insights

* Several products generated significant revenue while also exhibiting elevated warranty claim rates.
* Warranty claims were concentrated within specific post-purchase time windows.
* Store performance varied substantially across years, with a small number of stores consistently ranking among top performers.
* Product sales behavior changed significantly across lifecycle stages after launch.
* Data validation uncovered substantial synthetic anomalies that could have distorted business metrics if left unchecked.
* Warranty claim KPIs became more reliable after implementing chronological validation filters.
